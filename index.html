WHY GAPJUMPER?

Nelson Madela once said, "Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world." We wanted to create this game to educate people about the Wage Gap for women. In 2012, the median earnings of American women working full time year-round was $37,791. American men earned a median income of $49,398. The gender wage gap has hovered at about 77 cents on the dollar

since 2007. Women start of earning less than men even at the age of 16. Why is this happening? The Wage Gap for women is still happening because women still aren’t a priority. Women have played a key role in economic growth which means there skill and mindset is necessary to development of our societies. Closing the gender gap can help create a more equal and better working society. The at-risk-of-poverty rate is around 22% for women over 65%,

compared to 16% of men over 65. We need to work together to create equal pay for everyone no matter there race or gender.

Girls Who Code

Girls Who Code is a national nonprofit organization working to close the gender gap in the technology and engineering sectors. With support from public and private partners, Girls Who Code works to educate, inspire, and equip high school girls with the skills and resources to pursue opportunities in computing fields.

Women Empowered

Women Empowered is an organization that connects, educates and supports women of all ages and backgrounds, and inspires them to give back to their communities through mentorship, networking, and volunteerism.

Women for Women International

Women for Women International has helped nearly 420,000 marginalized women in countries affected by war and conflict. We directly work with women in 8 countries offering support, tools, and access to life-changing skills to move from crisis and poverty to stability and economic self-sufficiency.
